Best India Tourism Offers - Exclusively for You!

Tajmahal
Majestically located on the banks of Yamuna River, this Wonder of the world was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a memorial for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Built in ivory-white marble, this mausoleum flaunts Indo-Islamic architecture and manicured gardens dotted with fountains, and capture beautiful sunset shots. Read More...

Lal Kila
Located in Chandni Chowk, Old Delhi, this red sandstone structure was constructed under the reign of Mughal emperor Shah Jahan for protection against invaders. It is known for its intricate Mughal architecture and floral designs. Read More...

Gateway of India
Located on the waterfront in Colaba, South Mumbai, this arch monument is Mumbai's prime landmark. It was built by the British to welcome King George V and Queen Mary during a royal visit in 1911. Read More...

India Gate
Located in the heart of New Delhi, this war memorial commemorates the Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it is a prominent landmark in the capital. Read More...

Hawa Mahal
Known as the 'Palace of Winds,' this five-story structure in Jaipur is built with red and pink sandstone. It has 953 small windows or 'jharokhas' decorated with intricate latticework. Read More...

Qutub Minar
This UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi is the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 73 meters. It was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193 to celebrate the victory over the last Hindu kingdom. Read More...

Mysore Palace
Also known as Amba Vilas Palace, this historical palace in Karnataka is a masterpiece of Indo-Saracenic architecture. It was the residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and is now one of the most famous tourist attractions in India. Read More...

Charminar
Located in Hyderabad, this iconic structure was built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of a deadly plague. It is famous for its four grand arches and minarets. Read More...

Meenakshi Temple
Located in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, this historic Hindu temple is dedicated to Meenakshi, a form of Parvati, and her consort Sundareswarar, a form of Shiva. It is renowned for its 14 colorful gopurams (gateway towers). Read More...

Victoria Memorial
Located in Kolkata, this large marble building was built between 1906 and 1921 in memory of Queen Victoria. It is now a museum and tourist destination showcasing the city's colonial history. Read More...
